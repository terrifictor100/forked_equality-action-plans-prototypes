60% of your organisation are women, but only 24% of the promotions in the last year went to women. This suggests there may be an unintentional bias towards men in the promotions process.

Women tend to apply for promotion less frequently than men. (See the evidence.) This means that they miss out on promotions that men are getting simply because they applied and the women didn't. By including everyone in the promotions process by default, you remove this difference and give everyone an equal chance to be assessed for promotion.
