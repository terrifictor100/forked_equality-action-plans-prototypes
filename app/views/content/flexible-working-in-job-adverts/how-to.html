Start by designing a part-time role in your organisation. Identify the tasks, responsibilities and expected outputs that you need to be delivered. Prioritise the most important ones.

Specify the skills and responsibilities needed — consider whether it’s realistic for one person to have all of these.

Review the list of tasks and think:

  • could the job be done differently from how the last person did it?
  • has there been any ‘job creep’ due to the previous person accumulating duties over time?
  • could some tasks be done more efficiently by other teams?

Consider whether the list of tasks could be cut down or split into 2 (or more) smaller roles.

If the role includes supervisory or customer-facing responsibilities and full-time cover is needed, consider whether there is an opportunity for another team member to deputise or job-share.
