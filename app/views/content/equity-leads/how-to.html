First, appoint an influential senior individual who cares about equality, diversity and inclusion. The role will be more effective if this is the case. Their time should be ‘ringfenced’ — this means they should have sufficient time, resources and support away from other corporate functions to undertake their roles.

A diversity lead should have subject matter expertise and experience working directly on diversity and inclusion initiatives. They need support from the executive team and access to date. This ensures that a more diverse and inclusive culture spreads throughout the organisation.

A diversity task force needs a diverse mix of representatives. They need support to develop solutions to diversity challenges and to spread those throughout the organisation.
