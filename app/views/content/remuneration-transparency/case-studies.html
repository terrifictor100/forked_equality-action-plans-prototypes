In 2013, the Institute for Employment Studies explored pay progression mechanisms — the process by which someone moves up their pay range. The purpose of the research was to increase understanding of how they work, and how they have been modernised.

Six organisations agreed to participate in the research, including the Met Office, Dixons Retail Plc, a county council, university, and a large unnamed financial organisation.

Five of the case studies had implemented change to their pay progression system within the last 5 to 6 years. The county council is currently negotiating a proposed change with its trade union. The finance organisation considered implementing change to its pay progression system but withdrew its proposals.
